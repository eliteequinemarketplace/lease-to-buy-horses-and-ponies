What Is a Lease-to-Buy Arrangement?

A lease-to-buy (also called lease-to-own or lease with option to purchase) is a horse arrangement where the buyer leases the horse for a set period — typically three to twelve months — with an option to purchase the horse at a predetermined price at the end of the lease period. Many lease-to-buy arrangements credit some or all of the monthly lease fees toward the final purchase price, making the arrangement financially advantageous for buyers who ultimately choose to purchase.

Lease-to-buy arrangements give buyers the opportunity to trial the horse through multiple show seasons, in different environments, and under varying conditions — providing far more confidence in the purchase decision than a standard one or two-day trial. For sellers, a lease-to-buy arrangement transitions the horse to a committed new home with an extended trial period that builds mutual confidence between horse, rider, and buyer.

Benefits of Lease-to-Buy for Buyers

The primary benefit of a lease-to-buy arrangement for buyers is the extended trial period — months of competition and training experience with the horse before committing to purchase. This extended period reveals the horse's true character in competition, how they handle travel and new environments, their soundness over time, and their genuine suitability for the rider's specific goals and program. Buyers who have trialed a horse for a full season have dramatically more confidence in their purchase decision than buyers who have trialed for a day or two.

Lease-to-buy arrangements also provide financial flexibility — spreading the effective purchase cost over the lease period rather than committing the full purchase price upfront. This can be particularly valuable for families purchasing a first show horse, juniors moving up to a more expensive horse, or adult amateurs making a significant investment in a competitive mount.

Benefits of Lease-to-Buy for Sellers

Sellers benefit from lease-to-buy arrangements through the security of a committed transition — a buyer who is leasing with the intention to purchase is more likely to take excellent care of the horse, invest in its training and show career, and follow through on the purchase than a buyer who has only trialed briefly. Lease-to-buy also generates ongoing income during the lease period and builds a relationship between the horse, the new rider, and the selling trainer that often benefits all parties long-term.

What to Include in a Lease-to-Buy Agreement

A well-structured lease-to-buy agreement should clearly specify the monthly lease fee, the purchase price and option period, whether and how lease fees credit toward the purchase price, responsibility for care and veterinary costs during the lease, insurance requirements, competition and travel permissions, and the process for exercising or declining the purchase option at the end of the lease period. Elite Equine Marketplace always recommends that both buyers and sellers work with a legal professional familiar with equine transactions to document lease-to-buy arrangements properly.
